Distinguishing small well-differentiated hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) from dysplastic nodules (DN) is frequently difficult, and the precise pathological diagnosis is a key issue in clinical management of the patients. However, the morphologic criteria for confident diagnosis of early HCC are still controversial. To improve the consistency of pathological diagnosis of early HCC, the International Consensus Group for Hepatocellular Neoplasia comprising 34 pathologists and 2 clinicians from 13 countries was convened in 2002, and the group met on several occasions until 2007.
